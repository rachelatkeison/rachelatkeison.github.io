About

A portfolio shaped by both engineering and music-making.

I build the kinds of musical tools I wish existed. My work explores what happens when software stops feeling like software and starts feeling like an instrument — expressive, intuitive, and alive in the hands of a musician.

Aureine voice

Bright, warm, curious, and future-facing.

I’m especially drawn to systems that can listen to musicians, understand something meaningful about their performance, and respond musically in real time. That includes synthesizer design, MIDI analysis, immersive performance spaces, responsive visual systems, and collaborative tools that make music technology feel playful rather than distant.

Lab Notes

Small notes from the way I think about building instruments.

This section makes the portfolio feel personal and alive. It shows curiosity, taste, and the kind of questions I like exploring as I build.

Observation

The best music software feels generous.

It should make a musician curious instead of cautious. I’m drawn to tools that invite exploration and reward play.

Observation

Expressive systems matter more than feature overload.

I care less about how many buttons something has and more about whether it responds beautifully to human input.

Observation

Visual design is part of the instrument.

In music technology, interface and feeling are not extras — they shape how a musician hears, learns, and creates.
